Aputure, one global home for the light that shapes the frame.

Art direction and design for the worldwide site of a cinematic lighting company.

Selected screens

Aputure makes the light that shapes how everything else looks — on film sets, in studios, across a dozen markets that each shoot, sell, and read differently. The website hadn't kept up. Built for one market and stretched over the rest, it met local teams with broken layouts and copy they couldn't read, and they said so.

The work was art direction and design for the global site: a single visual language confident enough to hold industrial hardware and cinematic ambition in the same frame. Product that photographs like equipment and reads like cinema.

It had to survive contact with the real world — Japanese and German strings, local payment, the regulatory fine print of every region — and still look composed whether the buyer is a gaffer pulling a fixture for tonight's shoot or a procurement lead signing for the studio.

Good lighting never announces itself. Neither should the page that sells it.
