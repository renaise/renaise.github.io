Sensitive Subjects, picturing the things that don't exist yet.

A studio for photoreal product imagery, built on an AI-accelerated pipeline.

Selected frames

Most product imagery is a compromise. A render farm, six weeks, a dozen rounds of notes — and a result that still reads as a render. For the companies moving fastest on hardware and AI, the picture is the bottleneck: it arrives after the moment it was made for.

Sensitive Subjects starts somewhere else. Photoreal images of products and technology — including the chip, the rig, the concept that has no physical form yet — made on an AI-accelerated pipeline that turns weeks into days. Speed was never the point on its own. The point is that a picture should already believe in the thing it shows.

Each frame lands on-brand and ready to run: a campaign key visual, a social cut, a slide in the all-hands deck. A finished image, not a raw render someone still has to dress. And it travels — a paid placement, a pitch, a broader brand refresh — sized and formatted on the way out.

The identity carries all of this without standing still. Navigation works from radial dials rather than a menu bar; the layout reshapes as you move through it. A mutable web for a studio that takes a different shape with every client — assembled, each time, from the collaborators the work actually needs.

Because the next thing is always easier to believe once someone can see it.
